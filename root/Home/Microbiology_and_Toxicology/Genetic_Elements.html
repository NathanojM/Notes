Genetic Elements

Created Wednesday 27 April 2022

@MISC @YEAR3 @MICROBIOLOGY

@Chromosome
@plasmids = small pieces of double stranded @DNA
@viruses @genome
@Transposable_element

@Polyploids have > 10 copies of their Chromosome ( usually @Prokaryotes )
@Oilgoploids have < 10
@Monoploids have only 1 (very few species)

Ecoli


@e_coli has 4300 protein coding @genes
The genes used in the same @metabolism pathway are grouped into @operons

E coli chromosome is a thousand times longer than the cell itself⬇️

Plasmids

@Plasmids are found in @archea and @bacteria
Can replicate independently from @Chromosome
Genetics for @antibiotic_resistance is often contained in the plasmid
Plasmids can be @low_copy_plasmids (few in cell) or @high_copy_plasmid (lots of copies in cell)

Gene transfer


@vertical_gene_transfer : genes transferred from mother to daughter cell
@Horizontal_gene_transfer is the swapping of plasmids between bacteria

Transformation

@Gene_Transformation

Plasmids are released and taken up by another cell. A cell must be @Genetically_Competent
Free @DNA is taken up by the cell

Transduction

@Transduction is the transfer of DNA between cells using a @bacteriophage @viral_vector @viruses

  1. Injects DNA
  2. Bacteria replicates DNA → codes for new proteins for new DNA
  3. New virus produced
  4. Viruses released when cell goes through @Lysis

A piece of host DNA can get incorporated into the viral DNA

Conjugation

@genetic_conjugation is gene transfer with cell-cell contact
The donor cell has a @conjugative_plasmid which allows it to 'catch' to another cell using a 'bridge' called a @pillus
It gets shorter and reels the other cell in like a fishing rod
DNA can then be transferred when the cells meet.

CRISPR

@CRISPR stands for Clustered Regularly Interspaced Palindromic Repeats
It is essentially a @Prokaryotes @immune_system

The DNA has a series of spacers known as the crispr array
When the cell encounters @viruses , it inserts the DNA into its own spacers so that it can recognise it again.

CRISPR can be used to cut and insert DNA in very specific places → @genetic_engineering
The @cas_protein can then quickly cut the DNA
