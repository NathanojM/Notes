Risk Assessment

Created Monday 11 April 2022

@MISC @YEAR3 @MICROBIOLOGY @Toxicology

@protection_goals


Different hazards are important depending on what it is you want to protect
You need to know the mode of action of the toxin to know how to cure the toxicity. This is more difficult for some goals than for others; it is difficult to study development in the @placenta , for example.

Risk assessment paradigm

@Risk @risk_assessment_paradigm

Hazard identification

  • What effects do you expect to see? @Neurotoxicity @genotoxicity @immunotoxicity ?
  • What is the likely outcome of these effects? Death? Reproductive issues? Developmental abnormalities ( @Thalidomide )
  • Are these effects relevant? Does the toxin affect humans or crickets? Is the toxin likely to end up near crickets? Is it being given to humans?

Hazard quantification

Use the @dose_response_curve to determine the acceptable level of effect
@NOEL = No observed effect level (the highest concentration of toxin where there is no effect)
@LOEL = Lowest observed effect level (the lowest concentration of toxin where there is an effect)
ADI = @Acceptable_daily_intake → the MINIMUM threshold
TDI = @Tolerable_daily_intake → the MAXIMUM threshold
EDI= @estimated_daily_intake which can be calculated based on the concentration found in food and the study of someone's diet.


There is some @uncertainty in these thresholds : age, species and quality of the study can all vary

Avoidable Not avoidable
Non genotoxic compound Use can be managed and used in pesticides and additives No management possible. Soil contaminants, metals, dioxins
Genotoxic compound Not allowed in food. No thresholds or ADI. No genotoxic pesticides or additives Use NOEL and LOEL thresholds.
     

Levels of unavoidable compounds should be @ALARA (as low as reasonable possible) or in a @virtually_safe_dose with an acceptably low cancer risk.
The @margin_of_exposure for genotoxins is much higher than for non genotoxins.

Risk characterisation

MOS = @Margin_of_safety (the gap between ADI and EDI should be >1)
@Formaldehyde is found naturally in some foods → the EDI shouldn't be > 20% of the TDI, but exposure from bamboo cups often causes a higher dose than this.

Environmental risk assessments

Use the same paradigm as human risk assessments

Environmental risk assessments can be @prospective_risk_assessments or @retrospective_risk_assessments

  • Prospective: risks are assessed before the compound is used. Used in @Pesticide and household cleaning products
  • Retrospective: assesses the ricks of chemicals that are already in the environment after they have been released. Used on contaminated sites and where there is a controlled release of matter ( @effluent or smoke from factories)

Hazards can be quantified using the @dose_response_curve , but which one? Insects are obviously more sensitive to insecticides than mammals → which curve do you use? Do you take an average?

This one shows that at concentrations left of the blue line, less than 5% of species will be affected by the compound, or that 95% of them will be unaffected.

Triads


Risk assessments can be done using a triad approach:

  • Chemistry: compare the concentrations with known hazard thresholds
  • Toxicology: perform lab tests with organisms and test the chemicals at the site for toxicity. Assess whether there is a real causal link.
  • Ecology: take an organism census
