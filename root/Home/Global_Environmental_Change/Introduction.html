Introduction

Created Wednesday 28 September 2022
@YEAR4 @GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENTAL_CHANGE

The Quaternary includes the last 2 million years

Reasons why @quaternary science is important:

  • It is important to understand the natural climate cycles to distinguish natural driven climate change from anthropomorphic driven climate change.
  • @uniformitarianism applies here. It is assumed that all the laws of physics that apply now did then (the present is the key to the past) but the past is also the key to the future
  • We can't be sure of where climate change is going if we don't know where it's been. Is modern climate change actually unprecedented or is it all part of the natural cycle?
  • Instrumental climate records only go as far back as the instruments were invented

Controversy of the Quaternary

There are many arguments for deleting the quaternary and extending the @neogene period to the present day:
The boundary for the quaternary is defined by climate cooling where all the others are defined by stratigraphic boundaries
Said cooling is very slow and there is no abrupt change
There is differences between the changes in the south and the changes in the north
The boundary conditions are not consistent with the other periods.

Climatic components

  • Earth
  • @atmosphere and atmospheric composition
  • @clouds
  • @oceans
  • @glaciers
  • Changes in solar radiation
  • Changes in @thermohaline circulation
  • @albedo
  • Topography
  • Biosphere
  • @Milankovitch cycles

The very large number of components and variables makes understanding the drivers of and responders to climate change difficult.

Different responders are not always in sync. Assume that the red line is the atmosphere and the blue is the ocean. If the world gave up fossil fuels today the atmosphere would respond much quicker than the oceans. If the oceans take thousands of years to react to the change then we have already committed to global warming.
the fact that they are not in sync also means that events are less extreme.

Mid Pleistocene Transition


The @mid_pleistocene_transition is an unknown event that happened before the @holocene which caused much more frequent climatic shifts. Something happened but what is was remains unknown. There are theories that it was the beginning of agriculture and clearing of land which caused the change.
How stable is the holocene? Does clearing a tiny patch of land impact the climate?

Proxies

@proxy_data is any evidence that provides an indirect way of measuring a past environment or climate. Proxies ≠ @archives
@Multiproxies are multiple different proxies joined together to provide a less biased and more realistic reconstruction and removes some uncertainty.

Issues with proxies

Often influenced by more than one environmental variable.
Resolution can be ‘blurred’ by natural processes.
Can have hiatuses in the record.
Location of proxies can be spatially limited.
Time consuming
Poor preservation
Fossil misidentification
Different environments only preserve certain proxies
Can be expensive
Assume relationship with climate/environment has been constant through time
Transportation, contamination
