Atmosphere

Sunday 17 November 2019
@FOPG @GEOGRAPHY @YEAR1

Atmosphere = "Gaseous envelope of air surrounding Earth and bound to it by gravitational attraction"

~100km thick (vs 6400 km of Earth's thickness)

Importance

  • Reasons why the @atmosphere is important:
  • Keeps planet warm
  • Weather and @climate regulation
  • Provides habitat on land and in the air
  • Allows @phtosynthesis to occur
  • UV filter
  • Traps gases necessary for life

Density

  • Highly compressible (unlike the oceans).
  • Atmosphere is thickest at the surface due to @gravity.
  • 98% of the mass is concentrated in the bottom 30km

  • Density is measured in Millibars
  • Thinnest at the poles

Composition

  • Composition is consistent up to 80km
  • Composition of dry air (weight without water vapour) is highly variable

Water vapour

The maximum Saturation Vapour Pressure is temperature dependent

→ More @precipitation in drier climates.
→ The polar regions are dry because there is little water vapour in the local atmoshpere
@Water vapour is a powerful @greenhousegas and carries latent heat around the globe (the @latent_heat comes from changes of state)

  • 90% of moisture in atmosphere comes from @evaporation
  • 10% released by @transpiration
  • a little from @sublimation

Amount of water vapour in the atmosphere remains ~ stable on a global average ∵ evaporation ≅ precipitation
Precipitation > evaporation over the oceans → sea levels are rising @sea_level

See water cycle
Hot air holds more moisture than cold air
@systems

Carbon

  • @Carbon doesn't directly cause global warming. Warming comes from the amplifications of the initial warming. Water vapour is a much more powerful greenhouse gas.
  • It is natural to have 180-280ppm of carbon in te atmosphere.
  • @human We have increased the concentration by 45% since the @Industrial_revolution.
  • There were ~410 ppm in 2018
  • Evenly spread over the whole @atmosphere
