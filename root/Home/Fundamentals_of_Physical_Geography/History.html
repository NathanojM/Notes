•The quantitative revolution
•The process revolution

<1600

Greeks + Romans had topographic maps
Calculated Earth circumference
Interested in man vs environment

1600s

Slow progress ∵ things got rejected that went against the church in Europe.
Copernicus and his Heliocentric model

Arab geographers took over and travelled much further
Work in China on triangulation → maps of China

Renaissance

  • New interest in geography and progress regained speed (but Europe was still behind the rest of the world)
  • Innovations:
    • accurate clocks
    • navigation (concept of longitude and latitude)
    • printing press
    • New continents discovered

  • Focus on real experience (you can only find the Southern Ocean if you go there)
  • Shift from authority (the Church) to experience
  • Pioneered by Al-Maqdisi
New lands + mapping → colonisation and exploitation easier

1900s

Realised that P/T fall with height → mountain oberervatories
