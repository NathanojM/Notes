Electron rules

@Pauli_Exclusion_Principle : no two electrons may have an identical set of quantum numbers (otherwise they would be in the same place at the same time)
Electrons can jump between shells but can't exist in between them.
Each orbital can only have 2 electrons.

The @Aufbau_principle states that the lowest energy shells have to be filled first (for the first 18 elements)
Orbials are filled with increasing n+azimuth⬇️.

  1. 1+0=1
  2. 2+0=1
  3. 2+1=3
  4. 3+0=3 (but these have the same value, so n takes priority and they are ordered by n. This is why scandium fills 4S before 3D)

@Hunds_rule says that every orbital in a subshell is filled with one electron before ANY orbital is filled with two.

The Lanthanides

The @lanthanides or @rare_earths are the important ones for geochemistry.

An atom is in its @ground_state if its in its lowest energy state where all the shells are filled.
