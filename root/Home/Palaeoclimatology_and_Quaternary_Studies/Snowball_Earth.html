Snowball Earth

Created Tuesday 13 October 2020
@GEOLOGY @PQS @YEAR2

  • @Snowball_earth ≠ @Slushball_earth
  • Snowball is the hypothesis that Earth was 100% covered in ice with no ice free areas at all
  • Slushball supposes that there were some ice free areas which would allow @photosynthesis to happen @debate
  • There are glacial deposits in all @palaeolatitudes (even in the @palaeotropics ) → @glaciers must have had a very large extent

The Neoproterozoic

4 things happened in the @neoproterozoic :

  • Low latitude glacial deposits were deposited
  • Re-emergence of @BIFs after a 1 Ga hiatus
  • @Cap_Carbonates (inorganically precipitated carbonates) rest conformably on glacial deposits (which is usually an indicator of warm climate) See Palaeontology:Fossils:Multicellular Life
  • Carbon @isotope excursions became erratic

LLGD

The neoproterozoic had 2 glaciations but it was not the first. There was ice extent during the @palaeoproterozoic
The Pelaeoproterozoic ice age was caused by a rise in oxygen → oxidation of → global cooling. Methane is a much more powerful greenhouse gas than
.isotope


There are 3 major hypotheses for @LLGD:

High Obliquity Hypothesis

The @High_Obliquity_Hypothesis suggests that Earth's axial tilt was higher than today. It has been calculated that the tile would have to have been >54° for the poles to be free of ice and @photosynthesis to occur.
The question remains how the Earth could get so tilted. There is no evidence of an impact, and no @mass_extinction to go with it
✅️Explains ice around the equator
❌️Doesn't explain the high latitude glaciation

Slushball Earth Model

Suggests that there was an ice free belt around the tropics which allowed @Algae to thrive
❌️The climate is @metastable (see Geography:Fundamentals of Physical Geography:Landscape Evolution:Equilibrium)
❌️Model needs the presence of a mountain range in Australia which has never existed
❌️Doesn't explain cap carbonates

Snowball Earth model

✅️East to explain climate
✅️Predicts the presence of cap carbonates and BIFs
❌️Problems for photosymthesis; if the world is 100% km thick ice how can life live? No evidence of a mass extinction in the Neoproterozoic

BIFs

Bifs are made of @heamatite and @magnetite

They reappeared briefly in the neoproterozoic when dissolved iron reacted with the increased oxygen in the ocean and precipitated out.
Can be compared to @varves - formed seasonally as oxygen production in ocean changes.

Carbon Isotope Excursions

  • In the @Carboniferous land plants became more widespread → more photosynthesis → more organic matter which was rapidly buried and became coal
  • Plants prefer C12 ≠ heavier carbon isotopes → C12 removed from atmosphere → atmosphere is has isotopically heavier carbon
