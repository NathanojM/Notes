  • Native elements:
    • Gold

    • Carbon (or one of its @polymorph s - Graphite and Diamond)

    • Platinum (most comes from igneous complexes in South Africa)

  • Non Native:
    • Sulphides

  • Carbonates
  • Phosphates
  • Tungstates

Diamond/ Graphite

  • @Diamond and @graphite are polymorphs of Carbon
  • Diamond is @metastable and wants to become graphite near the surface.
  • 📖️https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metastability

  • The greener zone shows where diamond forms before it turns into graphite
  • Diamond can be an @accessory_mineral of @Kimberlite , named after the Kimberley Mine in South Africa (where diamonds are mined by drilling into the volcanic conduit)
  • Hardest known mineral
  • The largest diamond ever found is the @Cullinan_diamond
  • It's strength comes from its strong @covalent bonds

Gold

  • Comes from hydrothermal vents (where it crystallises out of hydrothermal solutions)
  • Comes from placer deposits (where veins have been eroded/ weathered, transported, and then deposited in lake beds.
  • 40% of Gold ever mined came from Uranium-Gold @conglomerates

Sulphides

  • Galena and Sphalerite often crystallise together in hydrothermal solutions
  • Significant ores for Lead and Zinc
  • Galena is mined for its silver ore in the Black Forest.

  • Chalcopyrite (a common copper ore) from hydrothermal vents, big igneous bodies, or @black_smokers
  • Its @alteration_products (what is produced after it has weathered) include @azurite and @malachite

  • @pyrite forms from hydrothermal ore deposits and in anoxic sedimentary rocks including mudstones or shales
  • Its alteration product in air is @limonite which is a @pseudomorph of pyrite.
  • Pseudomorph ≠≠ polymorph; its composition changes but the structure stays the same.

  • Others include Orpiment, Realgat, Stibnite and Cinnabar

Ores:

  • An @ore is a mineral aggregate with a component that is financially worth extracting.

  • Most ores come from @hydrothermal_vein_deposits

  • Some are from @volcanic_hosted_massive_sulphide_deposits which include a number of sources:
    • Black smokers
    • Where minerals leach out of rocks under @volcanoes and ppt S minerals
    • @Ophiolites (a section of oceanic crust that has been uplifted above @sea_level

  • Others come from @disseminated_copper_deposits which are found in @Subduction zones
