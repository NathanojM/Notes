Fault Rocks and Joints

Created Wednesday 17 June 2020
@GEOLOGY @ESH @YEAR1

@Fault_Rock_Classification

  • @Fault @Breccia is material that has been broken up by the fault, often by friction.
  • @Gouge is essentially the same as fault breccia, but much finer.
  • Fault breccia can become Gouge over time.
  • The breccia/ gouge tells you exactly where the fault is
  • @Cataclasis is the grinding up of material

@Incohesive material is loosely held together, while @cohesive material will need a hammer and chisel to get apart.
Material can have @foliation.

  • @Crackle_Breccias are the first stage, and fit together like a jigsaw⬇️
  • Followed by @Mozaic_Breccia where there is more cement in between.
  • @Chaotic_breccias is last, where they have been rotated and all that jazz.
  • A @Vein is a fracture that has been in filled with cement.

Pseudotachylites

@Pseudotachlytes form by frictional melting from the fault. They cool quickly → glassy

Cataclasites

  • @Cataclasites are bands of brittle deformation. The grain size reduces because the rock is breaking down, as the fault starts to put strain on the grains.

  • They start out as undeformed sediments breaking down into smaller grains.
  • @Protocataclasite comes first, where you can still make out the individual grains, followed by @Mesocataclasites, where you can see that 'there might have been grains there once'.
  • In @Ultracataclasites you would not be able to identify the original rock.

Mylonites

  • @Mylonite forms deeper where everything is more plastic.
  • Essentially the same as cataclasites, but by plastic deformation ≠ brittle.
  • Usually foliated
  • A @Blastomylonite is a mylonite that has got to the stage where minerals have begun to regrow.

Slickensides

@Slickensides are shiny surfaces on faults, caused by mineralisation, and the gouging of the cement.


Fibrous growths ⬆️ tell you the direction of movement, and which way the fault block was moving, but only preserves the last movement, and nothing before.

Joints

@Joints

  • @Transverse_Extension_Joints are parallel to the @Fold_hinge or prependicular to @σ3 (1)
  • @Lonitudinal_extension_joints are on the outside of the arc. The outer arc extends while the inner arc contracts, like breaking a Mars bar. (2)
  • @Diagonal_Conjugates form at 30° to @σ1 (3)
  • These can be used to calculate where the σs are/ were.

@Pinnate_Joints form at 60° to the main fault.⬆️

@Pressure_release_joints form when rocks get buried → stress. The rocks on top then get eroded, and the stress is released → joints parallel to topographic surface.

Columnar joints

@Columnar_joints / @Cooling_joints form when igneous rocks cool and contract. Everything cools at the same time → a tessellating pattern. @Giants_Causeway

Deformation Bands

@Deformation_bands are faults with faults with tiny amounts of displacement, but have gone through cataclasis. Because the grain size is reducing, it becomes more resistant to weathering → sticks out.
