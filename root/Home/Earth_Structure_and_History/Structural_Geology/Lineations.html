Lineations

Created Monday 06 July 2020
@GEOLOGY @ESH @YEAR1

A @lineation is a 1D feature or anything very elongated.
It indicates the direction of deformation

Fold axes

In @Chevron_fold the @fold_axis becomes a linearisation

Even though the whole fold can't be seen, the lineations indicate the folds:

Plane Intersection

When any 2 planes intersect, a lineation forms at that point

Dimensional Elongation

@Dimensional_elongation forms stretched features from exntensional/ compressional stress. The long axis indicates minimum compression.


@Rodding happens when minerals grow into rod shapes

@Mullions are like @boudinage (when the rocks grow into sausage shapes)

@mineral_lineation occurs when the stress is primarily in one direction, or it can form from flow.

@boudinage happens when a whole bed gets stretched into regular units ≠ just thinning vertically. Common where there are @Competency differences; the competent rocks become @boudins and anything less fills in the space around them.
Easy to identify the extension direction.

En Echelon Tension Gash Arrays

@en_echellon_tension_gash_arrays

@en_echellon = all in the same orientation but overlapping
tension = stress in rock
@gash = gash in rock
@gash_arrays = set of gashes

Fractures open perpendicular to @σ1
They can only open in the direction of @σ3



The compression plane mobilises minerals in @pressure_solution
The material that was in the fractures gets precipitated into the new fractures as cement. This is always brittle deformation ≠ ductile.

The mineral @Vein tells you the stress directions

Gash growth


  1. Gash opens and gets filled with minerals
  2. Gash cracks some more
  3. Crack gets filled with more minerals
  4. Repeat



  1. Gash
  2. Gash rotates under shear, but it can't open in any other direction other than σ3
  3. Rotates more into a sigmoidal shape (like @δ_clasts)

It is the tip that tells you where σ3 is.

This is like the @Helicitic_texture in metamorphic rocks.
