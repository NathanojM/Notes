Isostacy + Gravity

Created Thursday 16 January 2020
@GEOLOGY @ESH @YEAR1

@Isostacy means 'equal standing'
Mass of continental column = Mass of oceanic column

Isostatic Hypotheses

@Pratt_Hypothesis
Areas of lower density project higher above sea level than areas of higher density

@Airy_Theory
Crust is a rigid shell floating on a denser liquid, assuming a uniform crustal thickness
Thicker parts of crust sink into mantle
Thinner parts rise up out of it

Flexural Isostacy

Crust bends and rebounds under heavy loads

This is why the @Hawaiian islands are sinking and @scotland is rising

Measuring Isostacy

Satellites are used for @measuring isostacy

⬆️Satellite speeds up when approaching excess mass but slows down for missing mass.
⬇️Satellites can be flown very near each other to measure each others movements with lasers

  • It is easy to measure @isostacy under the oceans because it is equivalent to @sea_level
  • A higher mass attracts more water by gravity → @geoid_anomaly

  • ⬇️The @Spheroid is the theoretical perfect spherical shape of the earth ≠ the @Geoid
  • Scientists can use the difference between the two to calculate isostacy.
  • The geoid is caused by the liquid inside the planet spinning, which causes bulging at the equator.
