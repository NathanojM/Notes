Geothermal Gradient



The mantle is poorer in radiactive elements, so its heat loss is less than in the crust, and conduction releases more heat than convection.

Continental and Oceanic heat loss

  • Global average heat loss from the continents is ~60 mw/ m^2
  • The coldest areas are the @cratons
  • Younger areas are warmer
  • There is more heat flow in the oceans, especially at spreading regions

Younger areas of ocean (i.e. those closest to the mid ocean ridges have a higher heat flow)
The heat flow reduces because the heat loss starts out as being convective, but becomes more conductive as the crust cools and gets further away from the spreadng ridge.
