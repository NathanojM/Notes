Anthropocene

Angelegt Tuesday 28 January 2020
@PATE @GEOGRAPHY @YEAR1

📖️Anthropocene: a Very Short Introduction chapter 1

There is much @debate about about whether the @anthropocene exists or not
Similar idea to the @Noosphere

Does it exist?


Yes

  • Holocene was a stable environment,but it is no longer stable
  • Human impact will be recorded forever with @plastic in the fossil record (which is how geological time is measured) nuclear fallout is recorded in gas bubbles
  • All the damage we cause: deforestation and soil erosion needs marking somehow
  • There is nowhere on Earth completely untouched by humans. Even @Antarctica "the last great wilderness" is polluted by plastic and air pollution is no different from anywhere else. → "the end of nature"? if there are no "wildernesses left"?

No

  • Very short interval - insignificant. And the presence and number of humans is temporary: the number of humans is said to have been reduced to 30,000 after a @genetic_bottleneck 70,000 years ago.
  • In all the origin stories, whether it's @God creating man or natural selection proposed by @Darwin, humans have played no role at all; it is just one species becoming another.
  • We were not the first intelligent species: earlier species were the first to make fire and make tools and our only difference is a different shaped skull and being weaker.

Not sure

  • Can't say until humans are gone
  • Where would you start it? @Industrial_revolution? the start of fire? When @God created man?
  • It should start at the industrial revolution: humans can live quite peacefully as part of the ecosystem (see article:Home:People and the Environment) until the @industrial_revolution
  • The Holocene was when vegetation began to be cleared on a large scale.
  • Maybe it hasn't begun yet; should it start when we begin to colonise other planets? Maybe it should stop when we discover other life in the universe.

Yes but it shouldn't exist

  • Calling it the 'age of humans' is a very human centric and gives the idea that we are the only important species,almost suggesting that we have a 'right' to change the world the way we are. Most other periods are not named after the dominant species: there is no 'dinocene' or 'bacteriocene'. Periods are not called 'stratographic' for nothing.
  • Humans are powerful, but with great power comes great responsibility. If we are so important and powerful, we should be able to look after the world and fix the damage, but we are hindered by money and popularity → we don't deserve to call it the Anthropocene
  • Creates the mindset that it's ok to do what we do: "it's OK, we're in the anthropocene - it's meant to be like this" → it should be called the "destructosphere"
  • A pre-copernican attitude that humans are the centre of the universe

The Noosphere


Part of a three stage succession:

  1. Geosphere: inanimate objects
  2. Biosphere: living world
  3. @Noosphere: new state of the biosphere that humans have created, in which intelligent activities happen


Mumford's Epochs

Proposed by @Lewis_Mumford

  1. @Eotechnic (the middle ages)
  2. @Palaeotechnic (the time of the industrial revolution) and
  3. @Neotechnic (later, present-day)
